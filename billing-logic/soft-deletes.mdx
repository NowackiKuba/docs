# Soft Deletion Architecture

A core architectural principle of the Billy platform is the strict enforcement of **Soft Deletes** for all critical financial entities.

## What is a Soft Delete?

When you call a `DELETE` endpoint (e.g., `DELETE /api/subscriptions/{id}`), Billy does **not** issue a `DELETE` query to the database. Instead, it updates the record and sets a `deletedAt` timestamp. 

Soft-deleted records are automatically excluded from standard list queries, but they remain permanently stored in your database.

## Why Billy enforces this

In billing and SaaS infrastructure, hard-deleting records is incredibly dangerous.

1. **MRR Reporting:** If you hard-delete a canceled subscription, your historical Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) metrics for past months will instantly drop, as the system can no longer calculate revenue from that deleted record.
2. **Audit Trails:** If you hard-delete a customer, you lose the ability to trace the origin of past invoices, tokens, and payments.
3. **Ledger Integrity:** Token ledgers require the preservation of all historical grants and deductions to calculate balances accurately.

## How it affects you

- **Customers:** Deleting a customer soft-deletes the profile. All of their active subscriptions are instantly canceled. Their historical invoices remain intact.
- **Subscriptions:** Canceling a subscription soft-deletes the entity. It transitions to the `canceled` state and sets `deletedAt`. 
- **Invoices:** Paid and Voided invoices are completely immutable and can only be soft-deleted. The *only* exception to the soft-delete rule is an invoice in the `draft` state, which has no financial impact and can be hard-deleted.

<Note>
If you require complete data erasure for GDPR compliance (Right to be Forgotten), please contact support to initiate a compliant hard-delete purge across Billy and Stripe.
</Note>
