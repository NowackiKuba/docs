# Dunning & Retries

**Dunning** is the process of methodically communicating with customers to ensure the collection of accounts receivable when a payment fails.

When a recurring subscription invoice fails to charge (e.g., due to an expired card or insufficient funds), Billy automatically coordinates with Stripe to execute a dunning recovery flow.

## The Dunning Lifecycle

1. **Initial Failure:** A charge attempt fails. Billy generates a `payment_failed` alert. The subscription status changes to `past_due`.
2. **Smart Retries:** Based on your project's `dunningRetryDays` settings (e.g., retry 3, 5, and 7 days after the failure), Billy attempts to charge the card again.
3. **Resolution:** 
    - If a retry succeeds, the invoice is marked `paid`, a `paid` alert is generated, and the subscription reverts to `active`.
    - If all retries are exhausted and the payment still fails, the invoice transitions to `uncollectible`. The subscription is either canceled or marked `unpaid`, depending on your Stripe settings.

## Integrating with Dunning

You should listen to Billy alerts (or webhooks) to restrict access within your application while a subscription is `past_due`.

<CodeGroup>
```typescript Restricting Access
const subscription = await billy.subscriptions.getByCustomer(userId);

if (subscription.status === 'past_due') {
  // Redirect user to the Billy Customer Portal to update their payment method
  const session = await billy.portal.createSession({ customerId: userId });
  redirect(session.data.url);
}
```
</CodeGroup>

<Tip>
It is highly recommended to proactively notify customers of upcoming expirations *before* dunning begins. Listen for the `invoice_upcoming` alert to prompt customers to update their cards early.
</Tip>
