# Strong Customer Authentication (SCA)

If your business operates in Europe or serves European customers, you must comply with **Strong Customer Authentication (SCA)** under the PSD2 regulation. This requires customers to physically authenticate payments (e.g., using a banking app or SMS code) through a process like **3D Secure**.

## How Billy Handles SCA

When an invoice requires SCA, the payment attempt will fail with an `authentication_required` status. 

Billy automatically traps this state:
1. The invoice status remains `open`.
2. The subscription status transitions to `incomplete` (for initial payments) or `past_due` (for recurring payments).
3. Billy generates a `payment_action_required` alert.

## Resolving SCA in Your App

When you detect an `incomplete` or `past_due` subscription, or receive a `payment_action_required` alert, you must direct the customer to complete the authentication.

The easiest way to resolve this is by redirecting the customer to the **Billy Customer Portal** or directly to the **Stripe Hosted Invoice URL**.

```typescript
const invoice = await billy.invoices.get(invoiceId);

if (invoice.status === 'open' && requiresAction) {
  // Redirect to Stripe's hosted invoice page where the user can complete 3D Secure
  return redirect(invoice.hostedInvoiceUrl);
}
```

Once the customer completes the 3D Secure challenge on the hosted page, the payment succeeds, and Billy automatically transitions the subscription back to `active`.
