# Data Privacy & GDPR

Billy is designed with "privacy by default" principles to help your application remain compliant with global privacy frameworks like GDPR (Europe) and CCPA (California).

## Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

Billy requires minimal PII to function. The only required PII is a customer's **email address** and **name**. 
Billing addresses are optional but recommended for accurate tax calculations.

<Note>
**Data Residency:** All core Billy platform data is encrypted at rest and in transit.
</Note>

## Right to be Forgotten (GDPR)

Because Billy employs a strict **soft-delete** architecture to protect MRR metrics, calling `DELETE /api/customers/{id}` does not physically wipe the user's data from the database.

If a user explicitly requests a GDPR "Right to be Forgotten" erasure:
1. You must scrub their PII from your internal databases.
2. You must update their Billy profile via `PATCH /api/customers/{id}` to overwrite their email and name with anonymized data (e.g., `deleted_user_123@anonymized.local`).
3. You must initiate a deletion request on the Stripe side to purge the underlying `cus_` record.

By anonymizing the Billy record, you comply with GDPR while preserving the anonymous ledger entries required for historical financial audits.
