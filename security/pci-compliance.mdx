# PCI Compliance

The Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS) applies to any business that accepts credit card payments.

## Billy's Role in PCI

**Billy never touches, transmits, or stores raw credit card numbers.** 

Because Billy sits as an abstraction layer above Stripe, all sensitive payment information flows directly from your user's browser (via Stripe Elements or Stripe Checkout) directly to Stripe's PCI Level 1 certified vaults.

Billy only stores references to payment methods (e.g., `pm_12345`) and card metadata (brand, last 4 digits, expiry month/year).

## Your Scope

By using Billy in conjunction with Stripe Checkout or Stripe Elements, your application qualifies for the simplest level of PCI compliance (SAQ A). 

<Warning>
**Never log card details:** If you build custom forms, ensure you use Stripe Elements. If your backend server ever receives a raw PAN (Primary Account Number), your PCI compliance scope instantly expands to the most rigorous and expensive level (SAQ D).
</Warning>
